Jonathan Harker travels to Transylvania to help Count Dracula purchase property, only to realize he is trapped in the castle. He discovers Dracula’s supernatural nature and barely escapes. Meanwhile, Dracula sails to England to spread his influence.

In England, Mina Murray and Lucy Westenra notice strange changes as Lucy becomes mysteriously ill. Despite help from Dr. Seward and Professor Van Helsing, Lucy dies and becomes a vampire. The group vows to stop Dracula’s growing threat.

The hunters track Dracula’s movements across England, destroying his hidden lairs. Mina becomes a target and is partially corrupted by his bite. Her psychic link to Dracula becomes both a danger and a strategic advantage.

Dracula flees back to Transylvania, hoping to escape pursuit. The group follows relentlessly, using Mina’s visions to anticipate his route. Their urgency grows as Mina’s condition worsens.

In the final confrontation, the hunters intercept Dracula’s caravan and destroy him at sunset. His death frees Mina from the curse. Peace is restored, and the group reflects on the courage that ended the ancient evil.

###Key Characters
Count Dracula — An ancient vampire seeking to expand his power beyond Transylvania.

Jonathan Harker — A solicitor who becomes Dracula’s prisoner and later a determined hunter.

Mina Murray (Harker) — Jonathan’s fiancée; intelligent, brave, and central to the group’s efforts.

Lucy Westenra — Mina’s friend whose tragic transformation reveals Dracula’s threat.

Professor Van Helsing — A knowledgeable doctor who leads the fight against the vampire.

Dr. John Seward — A physician and Lucy’s suitor, providing medical insight and resolve.

Quincey Morris & Arthur Holmwood — Loyal companions who join the hunt with courage.

###Major Themes
Good vs. Evil — The novel frames the hunt for Dracula as a moral struggle between light and darkness.

Modernity vs. Superstition — Science, technology, and rationality clash with ancient supernatural forces.

Fear of the Unknown — Dracula embodies Victorian anxieties about foreign influence and hidden dangers.

Gender and Power — Mina and Lucy’s roles highlight Victorian expectations and the threat of transgression.

Isolation and Corruption — Dracula preys on the vulnerable, showing how evil spreads through secrecy.
